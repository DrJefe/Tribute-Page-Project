Tribute to:

His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama of Tibet

"If you want others to be happy, practice compassion. If you want to be happy, practice compassion."

Dalai Lama
  • 1935 - Born as Lhamo Thondup on July 6th, in the small hamlet of Taktser.
  • 1937 - Monks led by Kewtsang Rinpoche search for the 14th incarnation of the Dalai Lama. Their search leads them to Lhamo Thondup aged 2 years old.
  • 1939 - Lhamo Thondup, his family, monks and fellow pilgrims journey to the capital of Tibet, Lhasa.
  • 1940 - Lhamo Thondup is taken to the Potala Palace where he is officially installed as the spiritual leader of Tibet and is officially recognized as the Dalai Lama. He assumes the name Jamphel Ngawang Lobsang Yeshe Tenzin Gyatso.
  • 1950 - In October, 80,000 People's Liberation Army cross the border from China into Tibet. As the Chinese army marches on Tibet, the decision is made to make the Dalai Lama the political leader of Tibet. On November 17, the Dalai Lama is enthroned as the temporal leader of Tibet at the Norbulingka Palace.
  • 1951 - Lacking international support from Britain and America, the Dalai Lama sends Ngabo Ngawang Jigma to Beijing to convince China not to invade Tibet. On the 23rd of May it is announced that Tibet has signed the Seventeen-Point 'Agreement' for the Peaceful Liberation of Tibet.

His His Holiness the Dalai Lama at the age of four at Kumbum Monastery in Amdo, Eastern Tibet.

"In the practice of tolerance, one's enemy is the best teacher."

Thousands of Tibetan women surround the Potala Palace, the main residence of the Dalai Lama, to protest against Chinese rule and repression in Lhasa, Tibet.

  • 1954-1955 - The Dalai Lama visits Mao Zedong and other Chinese leaders in Beijing.
  • 1959 - With increased reports of unrest and fearing for his safety, on the 17th of March the Dalai Lama decides to escape from Tibet. Disguised as a common soldier he evaded detection and left Lhasa. On March 31st, the group of refugees reach the Indian border. On April 20th, the Dalai Lama meets with Indian Prime Minister and discusses the rehabilitation and resettlement of the Tibetan people.
  • 1963 - The Dalai Lama presents a draft of the democratic constitution aimed at democratzing the Tibetan administration. The charter enshrines freedom of speech, belief, assembly and movement.
  • 1987 - The Dalai proposes a Five-Point Peace Plan for Tibet in his address to the United States Congress.
  • 1989 - He is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for the non-violent struggle for the liberation of Tibet. In his speech he remains optimistic for a peaceful resolution to the conflict between Tibet and China.
  • 2011 - Having served as the head of the Tibetan state for over 60 years, the Dalai Lama requests the Tibetan Parliament-in-exile to relieve him of his temporal authority. He intendes to resume the status of the first four Dalai Lamas in concerning himself only with spiritual affairs
